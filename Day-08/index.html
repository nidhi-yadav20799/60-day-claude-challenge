      📌 Anomaly & Surprise

      Biggest anomaly: Jaipur currently ranks worse than New Delhi — a seasonal reversal, since Delhi's worst readings usually appear in winter inversion months (Nov–Jan), not monsoon season.
      Most surprising: Ahmedabad and Hyderabad post near-identical AQI (58) despite sitting 900km apart in different climate zones and states — pointing to shared vehicular/industrial emission patterns rather than geography driving air quality.

      ✅ Recommended Actions (National View)

      Prioritize N95 masks and indoor purifiers in Jaipur, New Delhi and Amritsar — all in the Moderate band with rising PM2.5 load.
      Cities with hard water (Bengaluru, Chennai, Delhi) should pair RO/UV filtration with a shower filter to cut hair-fall and scalp irritation risk.
      Pune and Mumbai residents can lean into outdoor exercise; still monitor PM2.5 during early-morning peak traffic hours.

                  Data & Methodology
                  Air quality (AQI, PM2.5, PM10): Live/near-real-time readings compiled from aqi.in, aqicn.org (World Air Quality Index Project) and IQAir, captured mid-to-late June 2026. Ahmedabad PM2.5/PM10 are directly reported station values; other cities' PM2.5/PM10 are derived using the standard US EPA AQI breakpoint formula from reported city AQI, since granular pollutant breakdowns weren't published for every city at the same timestamp — treat these as close estimates, not station readings.
                  AQI category bands: Mapped to India's CPCB National AQI scale (Good 0–50, Satisfactory 51–100, Moderate 101–200, Poor 201–300, Very Poor 301–400, Severe 401–500), applied to the sourced AQI values for consistency with Indian public-health communication.
                  Water Quality Score: A composite index (0–100) built from the 2019 BIS/PIB 11-parameter drinking-water compliance study across Indian state capitals (Mumbai passed all parameters; Delhi failed all) combined with 2026 city-level water hardness/TDS reporting. No unified real-time water-quality API exists per Indian city, so this score is directional, not a live sensor reading.
                  Data cleaning: Conflicting same-city AQI values across sources/timestamps were resolved by taking the most recent consistent snapshot; missing pollutant fields were estimated via the documented EPA formula above rather than left blank.
                  This dashboard is for personal awareness and general education. It is not medical advice — consult a doctor or dermatologist for individual health or skin/hair concerns.
