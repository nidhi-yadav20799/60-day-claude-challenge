### 90-Day Practical Roadmap

**Days 1–30: Close the highest-frequency interview gaps**
- Week 1–2: SQL depth — window functions, joins, subqueries, CTEs. Practice daily on HackerRank/LeetCode SQL until timed problems feel routine.
- Week 2–3: Cloud fundamentals — complete AWS Cloud Practitioner *or* Microsoft Azure Fundamentals (AZ-900). Pick one based on which target companies (Accenture/Capgemini lean AWS-heavy client work; IBM/Cognizant often reference Azure and IBM Cloud).
- Week 4: Statistics refresh — hypothesis testing, p-values, confidence intervals, and basic A/B test design, applied to one of your existing datasets (e.g., re-analyze the Superstore data with a simple hypothesis test on discount impact).

**Days 31–60: Convert existing projects into deployment-ready work**
- Week 5–6: Learn Flask or FastAPI basics; wrap one existing model (ChurnShield or CropYield Predictor) in a simple API endpoint.
- Week 7: Learn Docker fundamentals; containerize that same API so it runs anywhere — this single project becomes a strong "deployment" talking point.
- Week 8: Deploy your Streamlit dashboard(s) to a free cloud host (Streamlit Community Cloud or equivalent) so recruiters can click a live link instead of viewing a screenshot.

**Days 61–90: Depth, breadth, and interview readiness**
- Week 9: PyTorch fundamentals — rebuild one small piece of an existing TensorFlow project (e.g., a simple classifier) in PyTorch for comparison talking points.
- Week 10: Big data conceptual grounding — understand Spark's role in distributed processing at a conceptual/interview level (hands-on not required for fresher roles).
- Week 11: Enroll in one company-recognized certification track relevant to your top targets (TCS iON or IBM SkillsBuild Data Science pathway).

### Strongest Achievements to Lead With
1. **Retail Insights** — 51,000+ records, regional profitability analysis, discount-impact quantification, Power BI dashboard for product performance. *Your strongest "business impact" story — always lead with the number.*
2. **InsightEngine (CODE-A-NOVA)** — RFM segmentation, churn prediction, and a recommendation system unified in one Streamlit application. *Your strongest "end-to-end ownership" story — shows you can combine multiple ML techniques into a single working product.*
3. **SilenEko (Sign Language Recognition)** — MobileNetV2 transfer learning with a real-time MediaPipe/OpenCV pipeline. *Your strongest "technical depth" story — proves applied deep learning and computer vision, not just tabular ML.*

### Best STAR-Style Stories to Have Ready
- **"Tell me about a project with messy or large-scale data."** → Retail Insights: cleaning and transforming 51,000+ Superstore records (2011–2014) before regional and discount analysis.
- **"Tell me about combining multiple ML techniques in one project."** → InsightEngine: unsupervised segmentation (RFM) plus supervised churn prediction plus a recommendation system, unified in one dashboard.
- **"Tell me about a computer vision or non-tabular project."** → SilenEko: image-based transfer learning combined with real-time hand-tracking for gesture classification.
- **"Tell me about an NLP project."** → Fake News Detection: TF-IDF feature extraction feeding classical ML classifiers, evaluated on standard predictive metrics.
- **"What exposure do you have to Generative AI?"** → Reference the Technmicra academic internship: prompt engineering, LLM fundamentals, and business applications of GenAI.

### Likely Interviewer Questions to Prepare For
1. Walk me through one project end-to-end — the business problem, your approach, and the outcome.
2. How did you choose which features to engineer for CropYield Predictor or ChurnShield, and why?
3. How do you decide whether a classification model is "good enough" to hand off to the business?
4. Explain the difference between clustering and classification — where did you use each, and why?
5. You've used both Power BI and Tableau — what's your experience with each, and which do you prefer, and why?
6. How would you handle a dataset with significant missing or inconsistent values? (Tie back to your EDA/cleaning work.)
7. What do you understand about prompt engineering, and how could Generative AI support a business analytics workflow?
8. What would you improve about one of your projects if you had more time or more data? (Turn this into a growth-oriented answer — mention deployment or cloud exposure as your current focus area.)
9. Why this company specifically? (Prepare one tailored, specific sentence per target company — don't reuse a generic answer.)
10. Tell me about a time you worked as part of a team. (Reference the SilenEko group project and your specific contribution to it.)
